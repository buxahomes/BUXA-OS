Status: Approved

Owner: Founder

Last Updated: July 2026

---

# Purpose

This document defines how content activities are planned within the BUXA Business Operating System.

A Content Calendar is not a publishing schedule.

It is the structured rhythm through which business knowledge is communicated over time according to business priorities and customer needs.

The purpose of the Content Calendar is to ensure that knowledge communication remains consistent, balanced and strategically aligned.

---

# Vision

BUXA aims to establish a predictable rhythm of knowledge communication.

Content should be planned according to business objectives rather than publishing frequency.

The calendar should reflect business priorities, customer journey stages and organisational learning.

Planning should improve consistency rather than increase workload.

---

# Calendar Philosophy

Business knowledge defines communication.

Business priorities determine focus.

Content rhythm creates consistency.

Channels distribute knowledge.

Customer interaction generates learning.

Learning improves future planning.

The Content Calendar exists to organise knowledge communication rather than manage publishing dates.

---

Planning should support business communication rather than content production.

---

# Planning Principles

Every content calendar should:

- Begin with business priorities.
- Reflect official business knowledge.
- Support customer journey stages.
- Balance long-term and short-term objectives.
- Strengthen customer confidence.
- Generate measurable business value.

Planning should optimise knowledge communication rather than publishing volume.

---

# Business Rhythm

Content should follow the operational rhythm of the business.

Examples include:

## Daily

- Customer education
- Product highlights
- Project updates
- Industry observations

---

## Weekly

- Buying guides
- Educational content
- Thought leadership
- FAQ expansion

---

## Monthly

- Case studies
- Product deep dives
- Market analysis
- Knowledge review

---

## Quarterly

- Strategic themes
- Industry reports
- Brand positioning
- Major campaigns

The rhythm should follow business priorities rather than arbitrary posting targets.

---

# Content Balance

A healthy content calendar should balance multiple content purposes.

Examples include:

- Education
- Product Knowledge
- Project Delivery
- Brand Story
- Customer Success
- Industry Insights

No single category should dominate long-term communication.

Balanced communication builds stronger customer understanding.

# Customer Journey Alignment

Content planning should reflect customer progression.

| Journey Stage | Primary Content Focus |
|---------------|-----------------------|
| Discover | Awareness and education |
| Understand | Knowledge and explanation |
| Evaluate | Comparisons and proof |
| Decide | Confidence and reassurance |
| Experience | Support and guidance |
| Advocate | Community and long-term relationships |

Planning should support customer progress rather than isolated campaigns.

---

# Calendar Validation

Before approving a content calendar, confirm:

- Does it reflect current business priorities?
- Does it represent official business knowledge?
- Does it balance customer journey stages?
- Does it strengthen customer confidence?
- Does it support long-term business objectives?
- Can learning improve future planning?

A calendar should always support business strategy.
