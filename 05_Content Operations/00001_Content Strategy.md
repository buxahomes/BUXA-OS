# Purpose

This document defines the content strategy used within the BUXA Business Operating System.

Content Strategy is not the planning of marketing content.

It is the strategic transformation of business knowledge into communication that supports customer understanding, customer confidence and business objectives.

The purpose of this document is to ensure that every content asset exists for a clear business reason and contributes to long-term business value.

---

# Vision

BUXA aims to become a knowledge-driven content organisation.

Business knowledge should be transformed into valuable communication that helps customers understand, compare and make confident decisions.

Content should educate before it persuades.

Trust should be built before transactions occur.

---

# Content Philosophy

Business knowledge is permanent.

Content communicates knowledge.

Communication creates understanding.

Understanding builds confidence.

Confidence supports decisions.

Business outcomes strengthen future knowledge.

Content exists to create understanding rather than simply generate attention.

# Strategic Objectives

Every content initiative should support one primary business objective.

Examples include:

- Educate
- Explain
- Compare
- Reduce uncertainty
- Build trust
- Support decision-making
- Strengthen customer relationships

Content should always solve a business problem.

---

# Customer Journey Strategy

Different stages of the customer journey require different knowledge.

## Discover

Objective:

Help customers recognise a problem or opportunity.

Examples:

- Educational articles
- Industry insights
- Inspiration
- Trends

---

## Understand

Objective:

Help customers understand products, systems and terminology.

Examples:

- Buying guides
- FAQs
- Product comparisons
- PDMS explanations

---

## Evaluate

Objective:

Help customers compare options with confidence.

Examples:

- Product pages
- Case studies
- Specification guides
- Sample box content

---

## Decide

Objective:

Reduce uncertainty before commitment.

Examples:

- Testimonials
- Delivery process
- Warranty
- Installation guides

---

## Experience

Objective:

Support successful project delivery.

Examples:

- Care guides
- Maintenance
- Project updates
- Customer support

---

## Advocate

Objective:

Encourage long-term relationships and referrals.

Examples:

- Success stories
- Community content
- Designer partnerships
- Customer education

Content should always match the customer's current stage.

---

# Strategic Principles

Every content strategy should:

- Begin with business knowledge.
- Support one business objective.
- Match one customer journey stage.
- Reduce uncertainty.
- Strengthen customer confidence.
- Generate measurable business value.

Content strategy should optimise customer understanding rather than publishing frequency.

# Knowledge-to-Content Matrix

Every content asset should originate from one official knowledge topic.

| Knowledge Topic | Primary Objective | Typical Content Assets |
|-----------------|------------------|------------------------|
| Brand | Build trust | Brand story, About page, Founder content |
| Products | Educate | Product pages, buying guides, comparison articles |
| PDMS | Explain | Process guides, inspection standards, project workflows |
| Website | Guide | FAQs, navigation content, resource hubs |
| Marketing | Inspire | Campaigns, case studies, thought leadership |
| SEO | Answer | Search articles, AI answers, entity pages |

Knowledge is organised by domain.

Content is organised by customer need.

---

# Strategy Validation

Before creating any content, confirm:

- Is official business knowledge available?
- What business objective does this support?
- Which customer journey stage is targeted?
- Does the content reduce uncertainty?
- Will it improve customer confidence?
- Can business value be measured?

Content without business purpose should not be created.
