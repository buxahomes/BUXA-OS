# Purpose

This document defines how business knowledge is distributed through content within the BUXA Business Operating System.

Content Distribution is not the publication of content across multiple channels.

It is the strategic delivery of business knowledge to the appropriate audience, through the appropriate channel, at the appropriate stage of the customer journey.

The purpose of Content Distribution is to maximise customer understanding, customer confidence and long-term business value.

---

# Vision

BUXA aims to distribute business knowledge rather than simply publish content.

Every communication channel should become an extension of the Business Operating System.

Distribution should improve access to knowledge.

Knowledge should reach the right audience through the right communication channel.

---

# Distribution Philosophy

Business knowledge creates content.

Content supports communication.

Distribution delivers knowledge.

Understanding builds confidence.

Confidence supports decisions.

Business learning improves future distribution.

Distribution exists to maximise business understanding rather than content exposure.

# Distribution Principles

Every distribution activity should:

- Begin with official business knowledge.
- Match one audience.
- Select the most appropriate channel.
- Support one business objective.
- Strengthen customer confidence.
- Generate measurable learning.

Distribution should maximise relevance rather than volume.

Each communication channel has a different purpose.

| Channel | Primary Purpose |
|----------|-----------------|
| Website | Authoritative knowledge |
| Google Search | Knowledge discovery |
| AI Search | Direct answers |
| LinkedIn | Professional thought leadership |
| Facebook | Brand awareness and engagement |
| YouTube | Education and demonstration |
| Pinterest | Visual inspiration |
| Xiaohongshu | Consumer education and trust |
| Email | Relationship development |
| Internal Knowledge Base | Organisational learning |

Channels should complement one another rather than duplicate communication.

# Distribution Timing

Knowledge should be delivered when it provides maximum customer value.

Examples include:

- Before purchase decisions
- During project planning
- During installation
- After project completion
- During long-term ownership

Timing should follow customer needs rather than publishing schedules.

---

# Distribution Validation

Before distributing any content, confirm:

- Is the knowledge accurate?
- Is the audience clearly defined?
- Is the selected channel appropriate?
- Does the content strengthen customer understanding?
- Can the distribution be measured?
- Will learning improve future distribution?

Distribution should always support business objectives.
