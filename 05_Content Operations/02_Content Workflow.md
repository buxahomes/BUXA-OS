# Purpose

This document defines the standard workflow for transforming approved Business Knowledge into scalable communication assets.

The workflow ensures that every content asset follows a consistent process, regardless of platform, audience or production method.

It applies equally to people, Artificial Intelligence and future autonomous agents.

The workflow protects quality, traceability and organisational learning while enabling efficient content production.

---

# Workflow Philosophy

Content production is not a writing process.

It is a knowledge transformation process.

The objective is not to publish more content.

The objective is to communicate Business Knowledge accurately, consistently and efficiently.

Every workflow begins with knowledge.

Every workflow ends with improved knowledge.

---

---

# Stage 01

## Knowledge Retrieval

Every workflow begins by retrieving approved Business Knowledge.

Knowledge may originate from:

- Brand
- Products
- PDMS
- Website
- Marketing
- SEO
- Customer Documentation
- Existing Knowledge Topics

Only approved knowledge may enter the workflow.

Unofficial information must not become communication.

---

### Deliverables

- Knowledge Sources Identified
- Related Documents Listed
- Knowledge Scope Confirmed

---

# Stage 02

## Topic Selection

The workflow identifies one clear Knowledge Topic.

Each workflow should focus on one primary business question.

Examples include:

- Why engineered flooring?
- Moisture control.
- Expansion gaps.
- Designer collaboration.
- PDMS inspection.
- Product comparison.

A focused topic improves clarity and future reuse.

---

### Deliverables

- Topic Title
- Audience
- Communication Objective
- Related Knowledge Topic

---

# Stage 03

## Research

Research expands understanding without changing Business Knowledge.

Research may include:

- Existing Content Library
- Customer Questions
- Sales Feedback
- Search Intent
- Market Trends
- Previous Campaigns
- Performance Data

Research supports communication.

Research does not redefine approved knowledge.

---

### Deliverables

- Supporting References
- Audience Insights
- Common Questions
- Communication Opportunities

---

# Stage 04

## Content Brief

The Content Brief defines the communication plan.

Every Brief should include:

- Objective
- Audience
- Core Message
- Supporting Knowledge
- Desired Outcome
- Platform Requirements
- Success Metrics

The Brief becomes the production contract.

---

### Deliverables

- Approved Brief

---

# Stage 05

## Outline

The Outline organises information before writing begins.

Typical sections include:

- Hook
- Problem
- Explanation
- Evidence
- Recommendation
- Conclusion
- Call to Action

A structured outline improves consistency.

---

### Deliverables

- Approved Outline

---

# Stage 06

## Draft

The Draft transforms approved knowledge into communication.

Drafting may be performed by:

- Founder
- Artificial Intelligence
- Future Content Agents

Drafting should follow:

- Content Principles
- Brand Standards
- Terminology Standards
- Writing Standards

Drafting should prioritise clarity over creativity.

---

### Deliverables

- First Draft

---

# Stage 07

## Review

Review verifies communication quality.

Review includes:

- Knowledge Accuracy
- Brand Consistency
- Terminology
- Structure
- Readability
- Duplication
- Platform Suitability

Review protects quality before publication.

---

### Deliverables

- Reviewed Draft

---

# Stage 08

## Approval

Only approved content may be published.

Approval confirms:

- Business Accuracy
- Governance Compliance
- Communication Quality
- Brand Alignment

Approval remains a human responsibility.

Artificial Intelligence cannot approve official communication.

---

### Deliverables

- Approved Content

---

# Stage 10

## Publishing

Publishing distributes approved communication.

Publishing includes:

- Scheduling
- Metadata
- SEO
- Platform Formatting
- Media Validation
- Final Checklist

Publishing should always preserve traceability.

---

### Deliverables

- Published Content

# Stage 11

## Performance

Performance measures communication effectiveness.

Metrics may include:

- Reach
- Views
- Engagement
- Saves
- Comments
- Shares
- Click-through Rate
- Enquiries
- Leads
- Conversion

Performance evaluates communication.

Performance does not evaluate Business Knowledge.

---

### Deliverables

# Stage 12

Learning may update:

- FAQs
- Product Documentation
- PDMS
- Website
- Brand Language
- Future Topics
- Knowledge Topics
- Content Library

Knowledge continuously evolves through customer interaction.

Publishing therefore strengthens the operating system.

- Strategy
- Governance
- Brand Positioning
- Final Approval

- Retrieval
- Analysis
- Drafting
- Classification
- Recommendation
- Metadata
- Consistency Checking
