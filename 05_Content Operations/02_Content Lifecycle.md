Version: 1.0

Status: Approved

Owner: Founder

Last Updated: July 2026

Document Type: Content Standard

---

# Purpose

This document defines how business knowledge continuously evolves through content within the BUXA Business Operating System.

Content does not have an independent lifecycle.

It represents different stages in the communication and evolution of official business knowledge.

The purpose of the Content Lifecycle is to ensure that business knowledge remains accurate, reusable, measurable and continuously improved through customer interaction and organisational learning.

---

# Vision

BUXA aims to create a self-improving knowledge ecosystem.

Business knowledge should continuously evolve through content creation, customer interaction, operational evidence and organisational learning.

Content should never become outdated business knowledge.

Knowledge should continuously become better.

---

# Lifecycle Philosophy

Business knowledge is created.

Content communicates knowledge.

Customers generate feedback.

Business learns.

Knowledge evolves.

New knowledge creates better content.

The lifecycle belongs to knowledge rather than individual content assets.

---

# Lifecycle Architecture

Every content asset follows one continuous lifecycle.

```text
Business Knowledge
        │
        ▼
Knowledge Creation
        │
        ▼
Content Transformation
        │
        ▼
Content Distribution
        │
        ▼
Customer Interaction
        │
        ▼
Business Learning
        │
        ▼
Knowledge Evolution
```

Knowledge continuously improves through communication.

---

# Lifecycle Stages

## Stage 01

### Knowledge Creation

Business knowledge is created or updated within the Business Operating System.

Examples include:

- Brand standards
- Product updates
- PDMS improvements
- Website standards
- Marketing strategy
- SEO knowledge

Knowledge becomes the source of future content.

---

## Stage 02

### Content Transformation

Business knowledge is transformed into communication appropriate for different audiences and channels.

Examples include:

- Website pages
- Buying guides
- Blog articles
- Videos
- Social content
- Presentations
- Sales materials

Knowledge is transformed rather than recreated.

---

## Stage 03

### Content Distribution

Content is delivered through appropriate communication channels.

Examples include:

- Website
- Email
- LinkedIn
- Facebook
- Pinterest
- Xiaohongshu
- YouTube
- AI Search

Distribution increases access to knowledge.

---

## Stage 04

### Customer Interaction

Customers interact with business knowledge.

Examples include:

- Reading
- Searching
- Asking questions
- Requesting quotations
- Downloading resources
- Providing feedback

Interaction generates valuable evidence.

---

## Stage 05

### Business Learning

Customer interaction and operational evidence are analysed.

Examples include:

- Frequently asked questions
- Knowledge gaps
- Search behaviour
- Content performance
- Customer objections

Learning identifies opportunities for improvement.

---

## Stage 06

### Knowledge Evolution

Business knowledge is updated based on verified learning.

Examples include:

- Improved documentation
- New FAQs
- Better explanations
- Updated standards
- New training materials

Knowledge evolves.

Future content improves automatically.

---

# Knowledge Refresh Cycle

Business knowledge should never be updated simply because content becomes old.

Knowledge should be refreshed only when supported by meaningful business evidence.

Examples of refresh triggers include:

- Repeated customer questions
- Product changes
- New regulations
- Workflow improvements
- Search behaviour
- AI QA observations
- Market developments

The refresh cycle should always begin with knowledge.

Content should automatically improve after knowledge has been updated.

---

# Lifecycle Principles

Every content lifecycle should:

- Begin with official business knowledge.
- Preserve knowledge integrity.
- Encourage customer interaction.
- Capture business evidence.
- Generate organisational learning.
- Improve future knowledge.

Content should always strengthen the Business Operating System.

---

# Content Status

Content may exist in different operational states.

## Draft

Knowledge is being transformed.

---

## Approved

Knowledge has passed Business QA.

---

## Published

Knowledge is available to customers.

---

## Optimised

Knowledge has been improved through evidence.

---

## Archived

Knowledge is no longer actively communicated but remains historically traceable.

Knowledge history should always remain available.

---

# Lifecycle Validation

Before updating any content, confirm:

- Has the underlying knowledge changed?
- Does customer evidence support the update?
- Has terminology remained consistent?
- Will customer understanding improve?
- Does the update strengthen business knowledge?

Content updates should always reflect knowledge evolution.
