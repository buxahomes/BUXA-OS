# Purpose

This document defines how Artificial Intelligence is governed within the BUXA Business Operating System.

AI governance is not the management of AI technology.

It is the governance of how Artificial Intelligence and people collaborate to execute business capabilities.

The purpose of AI Governance is to ensure that every AI-assisted activity remains aligned with official business knowledge, business standards and human accountability.

---

# Vision

Artificial Intelligence should become a trusted digital workforce operating under one consistent governance framework.

Every AI capability should support business execution while remaining transparent, accountable and aligned with the Business Operating System.

Technology may evolve.

Business governance remains consistent.

---

# Governance Philosophy

Business knowledge defines authority.

Business standards define behaviour.

Artificial Intelligence supports execution.

People provide judgement.

People remain accountable.

Governance protects consistency, quality and long-term business trust.

---

# Governance Principles

Every AI capability should follow these principles.

## Business Knowledge First

Artificial Intelligence should always retrieve official business knowledge before generating outputs.

---

## Human Accountability

People remain responsible for every business decision.

Artificial Intelligence supports execution.

---

## Transparency

AI-assisted work should be understandable and reviewable.

Business decisions should never become "black box" outputs.

---

## Consistency

Artificial Intelligence should follow the same business standards regardless of platform or task.

---

## Continuous Learning

Every AI interaction should improve future business knowledge and future business execution.

---

## Customer Confidence

Artificial Intelligence should strengthen customer confidence rather than simply increase efficiency.

---

# Human Responsibilities

People remain responsible for:

- Business strategy
- Governance
- Product decisions
- Customer commitments
- Final approval
- Exception handling
- Continuous improvement

Human judgement cannot be delegated.

---

# AI Responsibilities

Artificial Intelligence supports:

- Knowledge retrieval
- Content generation
- Business analysis
- Workflow assistance
- Pattern recognition
- Recommendation generation
- Operational efficiency

Artificial Intelligence does not own business decisions.

---

# Decision Authority

Decision authority remains with people.

Artificial Intelligence may:

- Recommend
- Explain
- Analyse
- Compare
- Organise
- Predict

Artificial Intelligence should never independently:

- Change business standards
- Publish official commitments
- Modify governance rules
- Approve strategic decisions
- Override human judgement

---

# AI Decision Boundary

Artificial Intelligence may assist with business execution, but it should not independently determine business direction.

The decision boundary should remain clear.

Artificial Intelligence may:

- Retrieve knowledge
- Analyse information
- Recommend actions
- Generate content
- Support decision-making

People remain responsible for:

- Strategic decisions
- Customer commitments
- Business ownership
- Governance
- Risk acceptance
- Final approval

The objective of AI Governance is not to limit Artificial Intelligence.

The objective is to ensure that responsibility always remains clear.

---

# Risk Levels

AI-assisted work may be categorised into different levels.

## Low Risk

Examples:

- Draft writing
- Translation
- Summaries
- Formatting

Human review is recommended.

---

## Medium Risk

Examples:

- Marketing content
- Website updates
- Product comparisons
- Internal reports

Human approval is required.

---

## High Risk

Examples:

- Customer commitments
- Product specifications
- Pricing
- Legal content
- Contracts
- Business strategy

Human decision-making is mandatory.

Artificial Intelligence provides support only.
