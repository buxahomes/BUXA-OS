Status: Approved

Owner: Founder

---

# Purpose

This document defines how different Artificial Intelligence platforms are used within the BUXA Business Operating System.

AI platforms are execution environments.

They do not define business knowledge, business capabilities or business standards.

The purpose of this document is to ensure that BUXA remains platform-independent while consistently applying official business knowledge across different AI technologies.

---

# Vision

The Business Operating System should remain independent of any individual AI platform.

As AI technology evolves, business knowledge, business capabilities and governance should remain unchanged.

Platforms may change.

The Business Operating System remains stable.

---

# Platform Philosophy

Business knowledge belongs to BUXA.

Business capabilities belong to the Business Operating System.

Artificial Intelligence platforms execute business capabilities.

Platforms are replaceable.

Business knowledge is not.

---

# Platform Independence

The Business Operating System should never depend upon a single Artificial Intelligence platform.

Business knowledge, governance and business capabilities should remain platform-independent.

When a platform changes, only the execution environment changes.

The Business Operating System remains unchanged.

Platform independence protects long-term business continuity and prevents technology lock-in.

---

# Platform Architecture

Every AI platform follows one consistent architecture.

```text
Business Knowledge
        │
        ▼
Knowledge Interfaces
        │
        ▼
Business Capability
        │
        ▼
AI Platform
        │
        ▼
Business Execution
```

The platform executes.

The Business Operating System governs.

---

# Platform Principles

Every AI platform should:

- Retrieve official business knowledge.
- Follow Prompt Standards.
- Respect AI Governance.
- Support Business QA.
- Preserve terminology consistency.
- Contribute to Business Learning.

Platforms should never become the source of business knowledge.

---

# Platform Selection

Different AI platforms may have different strengths.

Examples include:

| Capability | Suitable Platform Characteristics |
|------------|-----------------------------------|
| Strategic Thinking | Strong reasoning and planning |
| Writing | High-quality language generation |
| Image Generation | Visual creativity and consistency |
| Coding | Software engineering capability |
| Data Analysis | Analytical reasoning and structured outputs |
| Automation | API integration and workflow execution |

Platform selection should always follow business objectives rather than technology trends.

---

# Platform Responsibilities

Every AI platform should:

- Execute assigned business capabilities.
- Follow Business Operating System standards.
- Respect governance boundaries.
- Produce explainable outputs.
- Support quality assurance.
- Preserve customer confidence.

Platforms execute work.

Business knowledge defines the work.

---

# Platform Validation

Before introducing a new AI platform, BUXA should confirm that it can:

- Access official business knowledge.
- Follow Prompt Standards.
- Respect AI Governance.
- Support specialised AI Agents.
- Produce consistent outputs.
- Integrate with Business QA.

Technology compatibility should never compromise business consistency.

---

# Multi-Platform Strategy

Different platforms may be used simultaneously.

Examples include:

- One platform for strategic reasoning.
- One platform for image generation.
- One platform for coding.
- One platform for automation.
- One platform for enterprise integration.

Every platform should operate from the same Business Operating System.

Consistency should remain independent of platform choice.
