# Information Architecture

Version: 1.0

Status: Approved

Owner: Founder

Last Updated: July 2026

Document Type: Information Standard

---

# Purpose

This document defines how information is organised throughout the BUXA digital ecosystem.

A clear Information Architecture ensures that customers, search engines and Artificial Intelligence all understand the same business structure.

The objective is to organise knowledge rather than simply organise webpages.

---

# Vision

The BUXA website should not function as an online catalogue.

It should function as a structured knowledge platform.

Every page should belong to a logical hierarchy.

Every piece of information should have one official location.

Every customer journey should follow a natural path.

---

# Information Philosophy

Information should answer questions.

Not create them.

Customers should always understand:

- Where they are
- What they are looking at
- What they should do next

Information should always reduce uncertainty.

---

# Design Principles

## Customer-Oriented

Information should be organised according to how customers think rather than how the business is internally organised.

---

## One Topic

One Page

One Purpose

Every page should have one primary objective.

---

## Progressive Disclosure

General information should appear first.

Detailed technical information should appear only when needed.

Simple before complex.

---

## Consistency

Information should follow the same naming conventions, terminology and page hierarchy across the entire website.

---

## Scalability

The structure should support future expansion without requiring major restructuring.

---

# Website Hierarchy

The website follows four information levels.

Level 1

Navigation

↓

Level 2

Category Pages

↓

Level 3

Content Pages

↓

Level 4

Supporting Resources

---

# Primary Navigation

The primary navigation represents the highest level of information.

Current structure:

```text
Home

Collections

Packages

Project Inspiration

Design Resources

Installation Guide

Care & Maintenance

Warranty

FAQs

Sample Box

Contact
```

Primary navigation should remain concise and stable.

---

# Content Hierarchy

Information should flow naturally from general to specific.

Example:

```text
Collections

↓

European Oak Flooring

↓

HD08 Natural Earth

↓

Specifications

↓

Installation

↓

Maintenance
```

Each level answers a more specific customer question.

# Information Relationships

Every page should belong to one primary category.

Related content may be linked through contextual navigation.

Example:

```text
HD08

↓

Related Style Guide

↓

Related Installation Guide

↓

Related FAQ

↓

Related Sample Box
```

The objective is to encourage knowledge exploration rather than isolated page visits.

---

# Navigation Principles

Navigation should always answer three questions.

Where am I?

Where can I go?

What should I do next?

Navigation should never overwhelm users.

---

# Internal Linking

Internal links should strengthen knowledge relationships.

Every important page should connect to:

- Related Products
- Related Resources
- Related Guides
- Related FAQs
- Related Collections

Links should provide genuine value rather than simply increase page views.

---

# Search Experience

Users should be able to discover information through:

- Navigation
- Internal Search
- Contextual Links
- Search Engines
- Artificial Intelligence

Every pathway should lead to the same official information.

---

# Information Ownership

Every page should have:

- One purpose
- One owner
- One primary category
- One canonical location

Information should never be duplicated unnecessarily.

---

# Future Development

Future versions may include:

- Designer Portal
- Customer Portal
- Project Dashboard
- AI Knowledge Search
- Interactive Product Finder
- Personalised Navigation
- Multi-language Content

without changing the core information architecture.

---

# Single Source of Truth

Information Architecture defines how knowledge is organised across the BUXA digital ecosystem.

Good information architecture reduces complexity.

Clear information builds confidence.

Structured knowledge creates a better customer experience.

Every page should contribute to one connected knowledge system.
