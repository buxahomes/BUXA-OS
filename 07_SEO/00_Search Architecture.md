# Purpose

This document defines the search architecture of the BUXA Business Operating System.

Search is not limited to search engines.

Search is the process through which business knowledge is discovered, understood and trusted by both people and Artificial Intelligence.

The purpose of the Search Architecture is to ensure that structured business knowledge remains discoverable regardless of future search technologies.

Search should connect knowledge with customer confidence.

---

# Vision

BUXA aims to become the most discoverable knowledge-driven flooring brand.

Customers should be able to discover accurate business knowledge regardless of whether they search through traditional search engines, AI assistants or future knowledge systems.

Search should become the natural pathway to customer confidence.

---

# Search Philosophy

Search begins with business knowledge.

Knowledge creates discoverability.

Discoverability creates understanding.

Understanding builds confidence.

Confidence supports better decisions.

Search is the systematic management of knowledge discoverability.

Optimising discoverability is more valuable than optimising rankings.

# Knowledge Discoverability

Knowledge discoverability is the ability of business knowledge to be accurately found, understood and trusted by both people and Artificial Intelligence.

Discoverability depends upon:

- Structured Knowledge
- Clear Information Architecture
- Consistent Terminology
- Semantic Relationships
- Entity Authority
- Accessible Content

Search engines may change.

Artificial Intelligence may evolve.

Knowledge discoverability remains constant.

The objective of the Search System is not to optimise algorithms.

The objective is to optimise the discoverability of business knowledge.

---

# Search Architecture

The BUXA Search System follows one continuous process.

```text
Business Knowledge
        │
        ▼
Knowledge Structure
        │
        ▼
Knowledge Discovery
        │
        ▼
Knowledge Understanding
        │
        ▼
Customer Confidence
        │
        ▼
Business Learning
        │
        ▼
Business Knowledge
```

Search continuously strengthens the Business Learning Loop.

---

# Search Layers

The Search System consists of six interconnected layers.

---

## Layer 01

### Business Knowledge

Search begins with structured business knowledge.

Examples include:

- Brand Standards
- Product Passports
- PDMS
- Website Resources
- Quality Standards
- Installation Knowledge
- Customer Experience
- Project Evidence

Knowledge defines what can be discovered.

---

## Layer 02

### Knowledge Structure

Business knowledge should be organised into a consistent structure.

Examples include:

- Information Architecture
- Metadata
- Internal Linking
- Structured Data
- Entity Relationships
- Taxonomy

Well-structured knowledge is easier to discover and understand.

---

## Layer 03

### Knowledge Discovery

Knowledge should be discoverable through multiple channels.

Examples include:

- Search Engines
- AI Search
- Website Search
- Voice Search
- Future Discovery Platforms

Search channels may evolve.

Business knowledge remains constant.

---

## Layer 04

### Knowledge Understanding

Discovery alone is insufficient.

Customers and Artificial Intelligence should be able to understand business knowledge accurately.

Understanding requires:

- Clear language
- Consistent terminology
- Logical organisation
- Context
- Evidence

Understanding creates confidence.

---

## Layer 05

### Customer Confidence

Knowledge should reduce uncertainty.

Search should help customers answer questions before making decisions.

Confidence is the primary objective of search.

---

## Layer 06

### Business Learning

Every search interaction generates new evidence.

Examples include:

- Search Queries
- Customer Questions
- AI Search Behaviour
- Website Search
- Content Gaps
- Search Performance

Search continuously improves business knowledge.
