# Purpose

This document defines how BUXA PDMS™ collaborates with Interior Designers throughout the entire project lifecycle.

The objective is to transform flooring from a material supply process into a professional project delivery service.

BUXA PDMS™ exists to help designers deliver their design intent with confidence, consistency and quality.

---

# Design Philosophy

Design Creates Vision.

Delivery Creates Reality.

BUXA PDMS™ bridges the gap between design and construction.

The Interior Designer leads the design.

The Flooring Project Engineer leads project delivery.

Together they create outstanding residential projects.

---

# Collaboration Principles

## Designer First

Within BUXA PDMS™, the Interior Designer is the primary project partner.

Every workflow is designed to support the designer's vision while reducing project risk.

---

## Respect Design Intent

The original design intent shall always be respected.

No material substitution, installation adjustment or construction change shall occur without designer confirmation.

---

## Early Collaboration

Successful delivery begins before installation.

The Flooring Project Engineer should participate during project planning whenever possible.

Early involvement reduces risk and improves project quality.

---

## Transparent Communication

Project communication should be:

- Timely
- Accurate
- Documented
- Traceable

Every important decision should be recorded within the project.

---

## Shared Responsibility

The Interior Designer is responsible for design.

The Flooring Project Engineer is responsible for delivery.

The Installation Team is responsible for execution.

Each participant contributes to project success.

# Phase 01

## Project Consultation

The Flooring Project Engineer works with the Interior Designer to understand:

- Project objectives
- Design style
- Flooring expectations
- Customer priorities
- Project timeline
- Budget considerations

The objective is to identify delivery risks as early as possible.

---

# Phase 02

## Design Review

The Flooring Project Engineer reviews the proposed design from a delivery perspective.

Review items may include:

- Flooring layout
- Installation direction
- Pattern selection
- Stair details
- Expansion strategy
- Transition details
- Skirting design
- Material compatibility

The purpose is not to modify the design.

The purpose is to ensure successful implementation.

---

# Phase 03

## Material Selection

BUXA provides professional support during material selection.

Support may include:

- Product recommendations
- Sample evaluation
- Colour coordination
- Finish comparison
- Technical specifications
- Installation compatibility

The final material selection remains the responsibility of the Interior Designer and Homeowner.

---

# Phase 04

## Technical Coordination

Before construction begins, the Flooring Project Engineer confirms:

- Approved drawings
- Material quantities
- Site readiness
- Installation sequence
- Construction schedule
- Quality standards

Potential risks are identified before installation starts.

---

# Phase 05

## Site Collaboration

During installation, the Flooring Project Engineer maintains communication with the Interior Designer regarding:

- Site progress
- Design clarifications
- Construction adjustments
- Unexpected site conditions
- Variation requests

Design decisions remain with the Interior Designer.

Delivery decisions remain with the Flooring Project Engineer.

---

# Phase 06

## Design Change Management

Design changes may occur during construction.

Every design change shall be:

- Documented
- Reviewed
- Approved
- Recorded

The Flooring Project Engineer evaluates the delivery impact before implementation.

---

# Phase 07

## Quality Verification

Upon completion, the Flooring Project Engineer verifies that:

- Installation complies with quality standards
- Design intent has been achieved
- Outstanding issues have been resolved
- Inspection records are complete
- Evidence has been collected

Where required, the Interior Designer participates in the final design review.

---

# Phase 08

## Project Handover

Project handover includes:

- Final inspection
- Project Evidence Package
- Maintenance guidance
- Customer confirmation
- Designer confirmation

The project is considered complete only after all required approvals have been obtained.
