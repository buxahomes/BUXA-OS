# Purpose

This document defines the official inspection framework used within the BUXA PDMS™ Quality Standards system.

It establishes how inspections are planned, performed, documented and verified throughout every high-end residential flooring project.

Rather than serving as a checklist, this framework defines the philosophy, structure and governance behind every inspection carried out under BUXA PDMS™.

Detailed inspection criteria, Control Points and acceptance standards are maintained separately within their respective documents.

---

# Vision

Inspection is not the final step of quality.

Inspection is the continuous verification of quality throughout the project lifecycle.

BUXA PDMS™ treats every inspection as a decision point that determines whether a project is ready to progress to the next stage.

Quality is built progressively.

Inspection ensures that quality is continuously verified.

---

# Inspection Philosophy

Every inspection should answer one question:

**Can this project confidently proceed to the next stage?**

Inspection exists to confirm readiness rather than simply identify defects.

The objective is to minimise uncertainty, reduce project risk and ensure consistent project delivery.

---

# Framework Objectives

The Inspection Framework exists to:

- Verify project quality objectively.
- Prevent defects before they affect later stages.
- Support structured decision-making.
- Standardise inspection procedures.
- Improve communication between stakeholders.
- Reduce project risk.
- Create traceable quality records.
- Build long-term customer confidence.

---

# Inspection Principles

Every inspection performed through BUXA PDMS™ follows the same principles.

## Prevention Before Correction

Potential issues should be identified before they become defects.

---

## Objective Assessment

Inspection decisions should be based on measurable standards rather than individual judgement.

---

## Evidence-Based Verification

Inspection results must be supported by documented evidence.

Photographs, measurements and written observations should accompany inspection findings whenever appropriate.

---

## Consistency

The same inspection standards should be applied across every project regardless of project size.

---

## Traceability

Every inspection must be recorded.

Inspection history should remain accessible throughout the project lifecycle.

---

## Continuous Improvement

Inspection results should contribute to improving future projects.

# Inspection Participants

Inspection responsibilities are shared across multiple project participants.

## Project Engineer

Coordinates inspections and verifies project readiness.

---

## Installation Team

Performs self-checks throughout installation and rectifies identified issues.

---

## Quality Inspector

Conducts independent quality verification based on approved standards.

---

## Interior Designer

Participates in inspections where design intent or implementation details require confirmation.

---

## Homeowner

Participates in final project acceptance during handover.

# Inspection Categories

Inspections may include, but are not limited to:

- Design Verification
- Material Inspection
- Site Readiness Inspection
- Environmental Inspection
- Installation Inspection
- Product Protection Inspection
- Final Quality Inspection
- Handover Inspection
- Aftercare Inspection

# Inspection Outputs

Every inspection produces one or more documented outcomes.

Examples include:

- Inspection Records
- Photographic Evidence
- Quality Reports
- Defect Records
- Rectification Requests
- Project Approval

Inspection documentation provides evidence that quality requirements have been satisfied.

---

# Relationship with Quality Gates

Every inspection supports one or more Quality Gates.

```
Inspection

↓

Quality Gate

↓

Approved

↓

Next Project Stage
```

Projects may only proceed after the relevant Quality Gate has been successfully completed.

---

# Relationship with Control Points

Each inspection consists of multiple Control Points.

A Control Point represents one measurable inspection requirement.

Control Points are maintained separately within the Control Point Library.

The library is designed for continuous expansion without changing the overall inspection framework.

---

# Relationship with Inspection Standards

Inspection Standards define how each Control Point is evaluated.

They establish:

- Acceptance Criteria
- Measurement Method
- Required Evidence
- Inspection Frequency
- Approval Conditions

Inspection Standards ensure that quality decisions remain objective and repeatable.

---

# Relationship with Inspection Reports

Inspection Reports document inspection outcomes.

Reports provide:

- Traceability
- Accountability
- Project History
- Quality Evidence

Every report should accurately reflect inspection findings.

Reports should never replace inspections.

---

# Governance

Every inspection performed through BUXA PDMS™ shall follow this framework.

Inspection procedures should remain:

- Consistent
- Objective
- Repeatable
- Traceable

Changes to the inspection methodology require formal review and approval.
