# Purpose

This document defines the official Quality Gate system used within BUXA PDMS™.

Quality Gates are formal approval checkpoints positioned throughout the project lifecycle.

Their purpose is to determine whether a project has satisfied all required conditions before progressing to the next stage.

Inspection provides evidence.

Quality Gates make decisions.

---

# Vision

Every project progresses through clearly defined stages.

A project should never move forward simply because work has been completed.

Progression should occur only after quality has been objectively verified.

Every stage must earn the right to proceed.

---

# Quality Gate Philosophy

Every Quality Gate answers one question:

**Is this project ready to move to the next stage?**

Quality Gates exist to reduce uncertainty, minimise risk and ensure consistent project delivery.

They prevent defects from being transferred into later stages where correction becomes more difficult and more expensive.

---

# Relationship with Project Workflow

Each Quality Gate corresponds directly to one stage of the BUXA PDMS™ Project Lifecycle.

```
Stage

↓

Inspection

↓

Quality Gate

↓

Decision

↓

Next Stage
```

No project stage should begin before the previous Quality Gate has been successfully completed.

# The Eight Quality Gates

## QG01 — Project Initiation Approval

Purpose

Confirm that the project scope, participants and objectives have been clearly defined.

Project may proceed to Design Review.

---

## QG02 — Design Review Approval

Purpose

Confirm that the proposed design is technically feasible and suitable for implementation.

Project may proceed to Material Planning.

---

## QG03 — Material Planning Approval

Purpose

Confirm that all approved materials, quantities and accessories have been verified.

Project may proceed to Installation Preparation.

---

## QG04 — Site Readiness Approval

Purpose

Confirm that the project site satisfies all installation requirements.

Project may proceed to Installation.

---

## QG05 — Installation Approval

Purpose

Confirm that installation has been completed in accordance with BUXA PDMS™ standards.

Project may proceed to Final Quality Inspection.

---

## QG06 — Final Quality Approval

Purpose

Confirm that all inspection requirements have been satisfied and any identified issues have been resolved.

Project may proceed to Project Handover.

---

## QG07 — Project Handover Approval

Purpose

Confirm that project documentation, customer acceptance and maintenance information have been completed.

Project may proceed to Aftercare.

---

## QG08 — Aftercare Activation

Purpose

Confirm that the project has successfully entered the long-term support phase.

The project lifecycle is formally completed.

# Quality Gate Inputs

Each Quality Gate is supported by objective evidence.

Typical inputs include:

- Inspection Results
- Control Points
- Photographic Evidence
- Measurements
- Technical Documentation
- Project Records

Quality Gates should never rely solely on subjective judgement.

---

# Quality Gate Outputs

Each Quality Gate produces one of the following outcomes.

## Pass

The project satisfies all required conditions.

The next stage may begin.

---

## Conditional Pass

Minor issues have been identified.

The project may proceed while corrective actions are monitored.

---

## Hold

The project does not currently satisfy the required conditions.

Further review is required before a decision is made.

---

## Fail

The project does not satisfy the required conditions.

Corrective action must be completed before reinspection.

---

# Decision Authority

Quality Gate approval should be granted only by authorised project participants.

Depending on the project stage, approval may involve:

- Project Engineer
- Quality Inspector
- Interior Designer
- Homeowner

Approval responsibilities are defined within the relevant workflow documentation.

---

# Relationship with Control Points

Each Quality Gate contains multiple Control Points.

Control Points provide the measurable evidence required to support approval decisions.

The number of Control Points may expand over time without changing the overall Quality Gate structure.

---

# Relationship with Inspection Standards

Inspection Standards define the acceptance criteria used to evaluate each Control Point.

Quality Gates reference these standards when making approval decisions.

---

# Governance

Quality Gates form the decision-making framework of the BUXA PDMS™ Quality Standards system.

They should remain:

- Objective
- Repeatable
- Traceable
- Evidence-Based

No Quality Gate should be approved without sufficient supporting evidence.
