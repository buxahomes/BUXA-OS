# Purpose

This document defines the official Aftercare Program within the BUXA PDMS™ Project Delivery Management System.

The Aftercare Program extends project delivery beyond installation and handover.

Its purpose is to preserve flooring performance, maintain customer confidence and strengthen long-term relationships through proactive support rather than reactive service.

---

# Design Philosophy

Project delivery does not end at handover.

Long-term quality requires long-term care.

Premium products deserve professional aftercare.

The objective of the Aftercare Program is to help every customer protect the value of their flooring throughout its lifecycle.

---

# Service Principles

## Lifecycle Support

The customer relationship continues after project completion.

BUXA PDMS™ supports customers throughout the lifecycle of their flooring.

---

## Preventive Rather Than Reactive

The best maintenance is preventive maintenance.

Potential issues should be identified early before they become major problems.

---

## Evidence-Based Service

Every aftercare activity should be supported by documented project information.

Historical inspection records provide the foundation for future maintenance.

---

## Professional Responsibility

Recommendations should always be based on professional judgement.

Maintenance advice should be practical, objective and traceable.

---

## Continuous Relationship

Every aftercare interaction strengthens long-term customer trust.

The objective is not simply to resolve issues.

The objective is to maintain confidence.

---

# Stage 01

## Project Completion

Upon completion, the customer receives:

- Project Evidence Package
- Warranty Information
- Maintenance Guide
- Cleaning Recommendations
- Contact Information

Customers should clearly understand how to care for their flooring.

---

# Stage 02

## Customer Support

Customers may contact BUXA regarding:

- Flooring maintenance
- Environmental conditions
- Cleaning recommendations
- Product information
- Technical questions

Support should be timely and professional.

---

# Stage 03

## Maintenance Guidance

Maintenance guidance may include:

- Daily care
- Recommended cleaning products
- Furniture protection
- Humidity management
- Seasonal movement
- Sunlight protection

The objective is to maximise flooring performance.

---

# Stage 04

## Service Requests

Where service is required, the Flooring Project Engineer evaluates:

- Reported issue
- Project history
- Installation records
- Inspection evidence
- Environmental conditions

The objective is to identify root causes before recommending corrective actions.

---

# Stage 05

## Project Review

Where appropriate, BUXA may conduct periodic project reviews.

Project reviews may include:

- Visual inspection
- Flooring condition
- Environmental assessment
- Customer feedback
- Maintenance recommendations

Project reviews support long-term quality.

---

# Stage 06

## Knowledge Growth

Every completed service activity contributes to improving the BUXA Knowledge Base.

Lessons learned may improve:

- Installation Standards
- Inspection Standards
- Project Workflow
- Customer Guidance
- Future Projects

Every completed project makes the system stronger.

---

# Flooring Project Engineer Responsibilities

The Flooring Project Engineer shall:

- Review service requests
- Assess project conditions
- Provide professional recommendations
- Record aftercare activities
- Update project history where required

Professional judgement remains essential throughout the project lifecycle.

---

# Digital Aftercare

Future digital services may include:

- Customer Portal
- Maintenance Reminders
- Digital Warranty
- Service History
- Maintenance Library
- AI Knowledge Assistant

Digital tools improve accessibility while maintaining professional support.

---

# Artificial Intelligence

Artificial Intelligence may assist by:

- Retrieving historical project information
- Generating maintenance summaries
- Providing knowledge references
- Organising service records
- Suggesting relevant guidance documents

Artificial Intelligence shall never diagnose project issues independently.

Final recommendations remain the responsibility of the Flooring Project Engineer.
