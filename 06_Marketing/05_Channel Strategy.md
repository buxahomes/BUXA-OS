Version: 1.0

Status: Approved

Owner: Founder

Last Updated: July 2026

Document Type: Marketing Standard

---

# Purpose

This document defines how business knowledge is distributed through different communication channels within the BUXA Marketing System.

Channels do not create knowledge.

Channels distribute knowledge.

Every channel should communicate consistent business knowledge while serving different customer needs throughout the confidence journey.

---

# Vision

The purpose of channel strategy is not to maximise exposure.

The purpose is to ensure that the right knowledge reaches the right audience through the most appropriate communication channel.

Every channel should strengthen customer confidence.

---

# Channel Philosophy

Channels are distribution methods rather than business strategies.

Business knowledge remains consistent.

Only the method of communication changes.

Marketing should adapt the presentation of knowledge without changing its meaning.

---

# Channel Architecture

Every channel follows the same distribution framework.

```text
Business Knowledge
        │
        ▼
Knowledge Transformation
        │
        ▼
Distribution Channel
        │
        ▼
Audience Interaction
        │
        ▼
Business Learning
```

Each channel contributes new evidence back into the Business Operating System.

---

# Channel Objectives

Every communication channel should support one or more of the following objectives.

- Build Brand Awareness
- Educate Customers
- Strengthen Customer Confidence
- Support Decision Making
- Build Long-Term Relationships
- Generate Business Learning

Channels exist to support customer understanding.

# Channel Categories

The Marketing System currently supports six primary channel categories.

# Channel Roles

Different channels perform different roles throughout the customer confidence journey.

| Channel | Primary Role | Confidence Contribution |
|----------|--------------|-------------------------|
| Website | Knowledge Platform | Build Understanding |
| Search | Discovery | Introduce Knowledge |
| Social Media | Engagement | Generate Interest |
| Video | Demonstration | Increase Confidence |
| Direct Communication | Consultation | Support Decisions |
| AI | Personal Guidance | Reduce Uncertainty |

No single channel should attempt to perform every role.

Channels work together to create one continuous customer experience.

# Channel Selection

Different channels support different customer needs.

| Channel | Primary Strength |
|----------|------------------|
| Website | Complete Knowledge |
| Search | Discovery |
| Social Media | Awareness & Engagement |
| Video | Demonstration |
| Direct Communication | Personal Guidance |
| AI | Personalised Knowledge |

Channels should complement one another rather than compete.

---

# Channel Consistency

Every channel should communicate:

- One Brand
- One Knowledge Base
- One Business Philosophy

The format may change.

The knowledge should remain consistent.

---

# Channel Performance

Performance should not be evaluated only by traffic.

Evaluation should also include:

- Customer Confidence
- Knowledge Engagement
- Quality of Interaction
- Business Learning
- Customer Questions
- Return Visits

Business learning is the highest-value outcome.

---

# Artificial Intelligence

Artificial Intelligence supports channel strategy by:

- Selecting channels
- Adapting knowledge
- Personalising communication
- Measuring performance
- Identifying opportunities
- Improving distribution

Artificial Intelligence supports execution.

Business knowledge defines communication.

---
