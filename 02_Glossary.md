# Purpose

This document defines the official business terminology used throughout the BUXA Business Operating System (BOS).

The Glossary serves as the enterprise ontology for BUXA.

Every business concept has one official definition.

All modules, documentation, Artificial Intelligence systems, automation workflows and business applications should use these definitions consistently.

The purpose of the Glossary is to ensure one shared business language across the organisation.

---

# Vision

BUXA aims to establish one authoritative business vocabulary.

Consistent terminology enables:

- Better communication
- Better governance
- Better AI performance
- Better automation
- Better organisational learning

Language consistency strengthens organisational consistency.

---

# Knowledge

## Business Knowledge

The authoritative knowledge that defines how the business operates.

Business Knowledge is the Single Source of Truth.

---

## Knowledge Domain

A structured collection of related Business Knowledge.

Examples include:

- Brand
- Products
- PDMS
- Website
- Marketing
- SEO

---

## Knowledge Asset

A reusable organisational resource derived from official Business Knowledge.

Knowledge Assets preserve organisational capability.

---

## Knowledge Evolution

The continuous improvement of Business Knowledge through verified business learning.

---

## Single Source of Truth

The one authoritative source from which all downstream assets, workflows and communications inherit their knowledge.

---

# Assets

## Asset

A governed, reusable business resource created from official Business Knowledge.

Assets create organisational capability.

---

## Brand Asset

A reusable asset that communicates the identity of the business.

---

## Product Asset

A reusable asset that communicates official product knowledge.

---

## Content Asset

A reusable communication asset derived from Business Knowledge.

---

## Sales Asset

A reusable asset that supports informed customer decision-making.

---

## Training Asset

A reusable asset that develops organisational capability.

---

## AI Asset

A reusable asset that enables Artificial Intelligence to perform approved business capabilities.

---

# Capability

## Business Capability

A repeatable organisational ability that creates business value.

Capabilities transform knowledge into execution.

---

## AI Capability

A specialised business capability performed by Artificial Intelligence.

---

## Core Capability

A strategic capability essential to the long-term success of the business.

---

# Products

## Product Passport

The authoritative knowledge source for one product.

Every downstream Product Asset inherits from the Product Passport.

---

## Product Data Standard

The official specification defining how product information is structured.

---

# Content

## Core Content

The primary communication created directly from Business Knowledge.

Core Content generates multiple Content Assets.

---

## Customer Journey

The sequence of stages through which customers interact with the business.

Communication should adapt to each stage.

---

# AI

## AI Workforce

A collection of specialised AI Agents performing approved Business Capabilities.

---

## AI Agent

An Artificial Intelligence system responsible for one defined Business Capability.

---

## Prompt

A structured interface between Business Knowledge and Artificial Intelligence.

Prompts communicate intent.

Knowledge provides authority.

---

## Knowledge Pack

A structured collection of Business Knowledge prepared for Artificial Intelligence.

Knowledge Packs provide context.

---

# Automation

## Workflow

A structured sequence of activities performed to achieve one Business Outcome.

Workflows coordinate execution.

---

## Automation

Technology that coordinates workflows according to predefined business rules.

Automation coordinates execution.

People remain responsible.

---

## Event Trigger

A business event that initiates a workflow.

Examples include:

- New enquiry
- Website submission
- Project milestone
- Content publication

---

# Governance

## Governance

The protection of Business Knowledge, consistency and organisational standards.

Governance preserves long-term business quality.

---

## Knowledge Integrity

The assurance that Business Knowledge remains accurate, consistent and authoritative.

---

## Version Control

The controlled management of changes to Business Knowledge and Knowledge Assets.

---

# Learning

## Business Learning

Verified organisational learning generated through execution.

Business Learning continuously strengthens the Business Operating System.

---

## Continuous Improvement

The ongoing refinement of Business Knowledge, Assets, Capabilities and Workflows.

---

# Customer

## Customer Understanding

The customer's accurate comprehension of Business Knowledge.

Understanding precedes confidence.

---

## Customer Confidence

The customer's trust in the business based on accurate understanding.

Confidence supports better decisions.

---

## Business Outcome

A measurable result created through Business Capability.

Examples include:

- Successful Project Delivery
- Customer Satisfaction
- Sales Growth
- Brand Trust

# Using the Glossary

Every Business Operating System module should:

- Use official terminology.
- Avoid duplicate definitions.
- Reference existing concepts before creating new ones.
- Preserve terminology consistency across documentation.

Artificial Intelligence should always use the official definitions contained within this Glossary.
