# Purpose

This document defines how recurring business activities are scheduled within the BUXA Business Operating System.

Task Scheduling is not simply the execution of time-based automation.

It is the coordination of recurring business capabilities according to the operational rhythm of the business.

The purpose of Task Scheduling is to ensure that recurring activities are executed consistently, efficiently and in alignment with business priorities.

---

# Vision

BUXA aims to establish a predictable business rhythm where recurring work is coordinated automatically while remaining aligned with business objectives.

Scheduling should support business operations rather than dictate them.

Business rhythm should improve organisational consistency.

---

# Scheduling Philosophy

Business events initiate responsive workflows.

Business rhythm coordinates recurring workflows.

Artificial Intelligence performs recurring business capabilities.

People focus on strategic decisions.

Business learning continuously improves scheduling.

Scheduling should support operational excellence rather than simply execute timers.

# Scheduling Validation

Before creating any recurring workflow, Automation should confirm:

- Is the activity genuinely recurring?
- Does it support a business objective?
- Can it be event-driven instead?
- Is the scheduling frequency appropriate?
- Is AI Workforce required?
- Is human approval required?

Scheduling should exist only when it adds business value.

---

# Event First, Schedule Second

Whenever possible, business execution should be initiated by meaningful business events.

Scheduling should be used only when no appropriate business event exists.

Priority should follow:

1. Business Event
2. Business Rhythm
3. Fixed Schedule

This approach reduces unnecessary automation while increasing responsiveness.

The Business Operating System should become increasingly event-driven over time.
