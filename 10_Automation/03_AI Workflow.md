# Purpose

This document defines how the AI Workforce participates in business workflows within the BUXA Business Operating System.

AI Workflows are not independent automation processes.

They are the coordinated participation of specialised AI Agents within standard business workflows.

The purpose of AI Workflows is to ensure that Artificial Intelligence consistently supports business execution while remaining aligned with business knowledge, governance and human responsibility.

---

# Vision

Artificial Intelligence should operate as an integrated Business Workforce.

Each AI Agent contributes one specialised business capability.

Business workflows coordinate these capabilities to achieve measurable business outcomes.

AI supports execution.

Business owns the workflow.

People remain accountable.

---

# AI Workflow Philosophy

Business events initiate workflows.

Business objectives define execution.

Business capabilities determine responsibilities.

AI Workforce performs specialised execution.

Business QA protects quality.

Human judgement governs exceptions.

Business learning improves future workflows.

AI should strengthen workflows rather than replace them.

---

AI Workforce performs business capabilities.

# AI Workforce Assignment

Before execution begins, the workflow should identify which specialised AI Agents are required.

Examples include:

| Business Workflow | AI Workforce |
|-------------------|-------------|
| Product Page | Website + Product + SEO |
| Collection Update | Website + Product + Marketing + SEO |
| Marketing Campaign | Marketing + Brand + SEO |
| Google Ads | Marketing + SEO |
| Customer Consultation | Sales + Product + Brand |
| Designer Proposal | Sales + Product + PDMS |
| Project Inspection | PDMS + Product |
| Weekly Business Review | Knowledge Manager + Marketing + SEO |

Each AI Agent should perform one clearly defined business capability.

---

# AI Workflow Orchestration

Workflow orchestration coordinates specialised AI Agents according to the business objective.

The orchestrator should:

- Identify required business capabilities.
- Assign specialised AI Agents.
- Sequence capability execution.
- Share relevant business context.
- Monitor workflow progress.
- Trigger Business QA.
- Escalate to people when required.

The orchestrator manages workflow coordination.

Specialised AI Agents perform business capabilities.

People retain governance and accountability.

---

# Capability Execution

Each AI Agent should:

- Retrieve official business knowledge.
- Load only relevant context.
- Execute one business capability.
- Preserve terminology consistency.
- Respect governance boundaries.
- Contribute to one shared business outcome.

Execution should remain capability-driven rather than platform-driven.

# Workflow Validation

Before a workflow proceeds to execution, Artificial Intelligence should confirm:

- The business objective is understood.
- Required AI Agents have been assigned.
- Official knowledge has been loaded.
- Governance requirements have been respected.
- Business QA has been completed.
- Human approval requirements are clear.

Workflow quality depends upon capability coordination.
