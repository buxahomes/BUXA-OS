# Purpose

This document defines how business execution is orchestrated within the BUXA Business Operating System.

Automation is not a collection of automated tasks.

It is the coordination of business capabilities through structured workflows initiated by business events.

The purpose of this architecture is to ensure that every workflow remains consistent, scalable, auditable and continuously improving.

---

# Vision

BUXA aims to build an event-driven business where business capabilities are coordinated automatically while remaining governed by business knowledge, AI governance and human accountability.

Automation should coordinate work rather than replace responsibility.

Every workflow should produce measurable business outcomes.

Every business outcome should contribute to continuous organisational learning.

---

# Automation Philosophy

Business events initiate work.

Business workflows coordinate execution.

Business capabilities perform specialised responsibilities.

Artificial Intelligence supports execution.

People provide judgement.

Business outcomes create value.

Business learning improves future execution.

Automation exists to coordinate business execution rather than automate isolated tasks.

---

Every automated workflow follows one consistent architecture.


# Architectural Layers

## Layer 01

### Business Events

Business events determine when workflows begin.

Examples include:

- New customer enquiry
- Website published
- Product updated
- Inspection completed
- Marketing campaign launched
- Customer feedback received

Business events trigger execution.

---

## Layer 02

### Workflow Planning

Workflow planning determines:

- Business objective
- Required business capabilities
- Required knowledge
- Human approval requirements
- Expected outcomes

Planning should always occur before execution.

---

## Layer 03

### Knowledge Loading

Only the knowledge required for the workflow should be retrieved.

Knowledge should originate from official Business Operating System modules.

Knowledge loading improves execution quality.

---

## Layer 04

### AI Workforce Coordination

Automation coordinates specialised AI Agents according to the workflow.

Each AI Agent performs one clearly defined business capability.

Collaboration replaces duplication.

---

## Layer 05

### Business QA

Business QA validates:

- Business knowledge
- Business standards
- Terminology
- Governance
- Customer confidence

Quality should be validated before execution.

---

## Layer 06

### Human Approval

When business risk requires human judgement, approval should occur before execution.

Examples include:

- Customer commitments
- Pricing
- Contracts
- Strategic decisions

Automation supports human decision-making.

---

## Layer 07

### Business Execution

Execution applies approved workflows.

Examples include:

- Publishing website updates
- Sending customer communications
- Updating project records
- Publishing marketing content
- Generating reports

Execution should remain consistent.

---

## Layer 08

### Business Outcome

Every workflow should produce measurable business outcomes.

Examples include:

- Customer understanding
- Customer confidence
- Project progress
- Marketing performance
- Sales support
- Operational efficiency

Business outcomes determine workflow effectiveness.

---

## Layer 09

### Business Learning

Every workflow generates evidence.

Evidence should improve:

- Business Knowledge
- Workflow Design
- AI Workforce
- Automation
- Customer Experience

Business learning continuously improves the Business Operating System.

---

# Automation Principles

Every automated workflow should:

- Begin with a business event.
- Follow standard workflow architecture.
- Retrieve official business knowledge.
- Coordinate specialised AI capabilities.
- Respect governance.
- Produce measurable outcomes.
- Generate organisational learning.

Automation should never bypass governance or business quality.
