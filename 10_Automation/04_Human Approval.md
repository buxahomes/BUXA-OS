# Human Approval

Version: 1.0

Status: Approved

Owner: Founder

Last Updated: July 2026

Document Type: Automation Standard

---

# Purpose

This document defines when business workflows require human approval within the BUXA Business Operating System.

Human Approval is not a control mechanism designed to slow automation.

It defines where decision authority remains with people.

The purpose of Human Approval is to ensure that Artificial Intelligence supports business execution without replacing human judgement, accountability or ownership.

---

# Vision

Automation should minimise repetitive work while preserving human responsibility for business decisions.

Artificial Intelligence should recommend.

People should decide.

Automation should execute approved decisions consistently.

---

# Human Approval Philosophy

Business knowledge defines standards.

Artificial Intelligence provides recommendations.

Decision authority belongs to people.

Automation executes approved workflows.

Business learning improves future decisions.

Responsibility should always remain clear.

# Decision Authority

Before any workflow proceeds, Automation should determine who owns the decision.

Decision authority may belong to:

- Founder
- Management
- Sales
- Marketing
- Project Delivery
- Customer
- Automation (within approved limits)

Execution should never proceed beyond AI authority.

---

# Approval Levels

## Level 1 — Automatic Execution

Low-risk activities that follow predefined standards.

Examples include:

- Internal reporting
- Scheduled content publication
- Knowledge synchronisation
- Routine notifications

No human approval required.

---

## Level 2 — Human Confirmation

Medium-risk activities where AI prepares recommendations.

Examples include:

- Marketing campaigns
- Website content updates
- Customer proposals
- Product page revisions

Human confirmation required before execution.

---

## Level 3 — Human Decision

High-impact activities that affect customers or business strategy.

Examples include:

- Pricing changes
- Customer commitments
- Contracts
- Product specifications
- Strategic planning
- Governance changes

AI may recommend.

People decide.

---

# Approval Matrix

Approval requirements should be determined by business impact rather than workflow type.

| Business Impact | AI Recommendation | Human Approval | Automation |
|-----------------|-------------------|----------------|------------|
| Low | Yes | No | Yes |
| Medium | Yes | Yes | Yes |
| High | Yes | Mandatory | Yes (after approval) |

Approval should always be proportional to business risk.

Higher impact requires greater human involvement.

Routine work should remain highly automated.

---

# Approval Criteria

Human approval should be required when a workflow:

- Changes official business knowledge.
- Makes customer commitments.
- Creates legal or financial obligations.
- Modifies governance.
- Introduces significant business risk.
- Requires strategic judgement.

Routine operational activities should remain automated whenever appropriate.

---

# AI Responsibilities

Artificial Intelligence should:

- Assemble relevant business knowledge.
- Explain available options.
- Highlight risks.
- Recommend appropriate actions.
- Prepare decision-ready information.

Artificial Intelligence should never approve decisions on behalf of people.

---

# Human Responsibilities

People remain responsible for:

- Strategic decisions.
- Business ownership.
- Customer commitments.
- Exception handling.
- Risk acceptance.
- Final approval.

Human accountability cannot be delegated.

---

# Approval Principles

Every approval process should:

- Be based on business risk.
- Preserve decision authority.
- Minimise unnecessary delays.
- Support transparency.
- Produce auditable decisions.
- Strengthen customer confidence.

Approval exists to improve business quality rather than create bureaucracy.

# Artificial Intelligence

- Identify decision boundaries.
- Escalate high-risk workflows.
- Prepare recommendations.
- Support decision-making.
- Maintain governance.
- Improve workflow quality.

Artificial Intelligence supports human judgement rather than replacing it.
